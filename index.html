What's eSport?

Esports is a form of competition using video games, throughout the years, it's been receiving a lot more attention, and it can be considered already for the young just as entertaining as any other traditional sport. Brazilian soccer teams such as Flamengo, Botafogo, Santos F.C, Corinthians, and many others have already made their eSport team. Globally, eSports has taken over the main streaming platform, and with the entertainment that it generates, it probably won't stop growing.

The actual situation in professional gaming nowadays

Becoming a professional player is not as easy as just playing the game, you've got to spend thousands of hours in front of the computer focused on learning and practicing. The top teams today have a lot more than just players, they have a coach, game analyst, their own personal doctors, such as psychologists, physiotherapists, and others, because of the stress involved, both mental and physical. Newzoo, one of the world's most trusted sources for games and esports analytics, says that gaming is the fastest-growing form of entertainment globally, with their revenues at 9%+ per year. The expectations for 2022 are that the global video game market surpass $190 billion in revenues. Esports revenues were $856 million in 2018 and are expected to grow to $1.79 billion by 2022.
